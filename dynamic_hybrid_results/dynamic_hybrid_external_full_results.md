# Dynamic Hybrid — portable variant — full results

This is the portable variant of Dynamic Hybrid: a per-query α predictor that takes a query and emits a scalar α in the `score = (1-α)·dense + α·bm25` fusion. It is designed to slot into any hybrid retrieval stack regardless of encoder, BM25 implementation, or index.

- **Per-query inference latency**: **0.40 ms** (402.9 μs).
- **Training**: `1` epoch(s), 920,229 training samples, τ = `0.005`.
- **α grid**: 21 points from 0.00 to 1.00 in steps of 0.05 (0.00 = pure dense, 1.00 = pure BM25).

### 6.3 Structural findings from the α sweeps

- **R@1 collapses as α rises**, on every corpus. Linear BM25 blending reorders top-ranked dense items with BM25 ties and destroys top-1 precision. Any fixed positive α pays an R@1 tax.
- **R@10 has a bimodal shape.** Dense is near-peak, with a secondary peak near α = 0.5 on FEVER and NQ where BM25 and dense ranks weight equally in the fusion. The grid sweep makes this visible.
- **Mean rank is the cleanest hybrid signal.** Dynamic Hybrid cuts mean rank meaningfully against dense on FEVER (~ 20 positions) and NQ (~ 18 positions), and on held-out FEVER and NQ by similar amounts. Mean rank captures reordering benefit below top-k that R@k metrics clip.
- **NCE favors Dynamic Hybrid.** On every corpus, Dynamic Hybrid NCE is well below best-static NCE (lower is better). Per-query α is better-calibrated across the full fused distribution than any single scalar α can be.

### 6.4 Held-out behavior

- Held-out dense baselines are already very strong (FEVER held-out dense R@10 > 0.96). The structural ceiling for R@10 improvement on these corpora is small.
- Dynamic Hybrid tracks dense within ~1 absolute point on R@1 and R@10 on every held-out corpus — while beating best static α on R@1 by tens of absolute points on the same sets. The portable variant does not overfit to training-set encoder axes.
- Mean rank continues to beat dense on FiQA, FEVER, and NQ held-out. On SciFact held-out, dense is near top-k saturation, leaving little room to move inside the top-K window.

### 6.5 Encoder-level patterns

- **fiqa/test**: best dyn − dense R@10 lift on `jina3` (+0.0320), worst on `qwen2` (-0.0064). Best R@1 lift on `jina3` (+0.0128).
- **fever/test**: best dyn − dense R@10 lift on `nomic` (+0.5416), worst on `mxbai` (+0.0000). Best R@1 lift on `nomic` (+0.3022).
- **scifact/test**: best dyn − dense R@10 lift on `jina3` (+0.0623), worst on `nomic` (-0.0172). Best R@1 lift on `jina3` (+0.0588).
- **nq/test**: best dyn − dense R@10 lift on `e5` (+0.3723), worst on `minilm` (-0.0030). Best R@1 lift on `e5` (+0.1489).
- **fiqa/dev**: best dyn − dense R@10 lift on `jina3` (+0.0380), worst on `e5mistral` (-0.0111). Best R@1 lift on `jina3` (+0.0292).

### 6.6 Bottom line — portable variant

- **Sub-millisecond inference.** 0.40 ms / query. At that budget, Dynamic Hybrid is additive to any hybrid retrieval path — it does not change the shape of the stack it sits in.
- **In-domain R@10 gains are real on lexically-rich corpora.** FEVER and NQ are where hybrid earns its keep; FiQA and SciFact are dense-dominated (the dense ceiling is already close).
- **R@1 is held against dense while fixed α collapses it.** Scalar-α fusion has a structural ceiling on R@1 reordering: any non-zero fixed α sheds 8 – 22 absolute R@1 points on the in-domain sets. Dynamic Hybrid stays with dense on R@1 across the board and wins tens of absolute R@1 points over best static α on the lexically-rich sets.
- **Mean rank and NCE are the cleanest signals.** Dynamic Hybrid improves both on every in-domain corpus and on held-out FiQA, FEVER, and NQ.
- **Generalizes to unseen encoder families.** On three encoders held out of training, R@10 and R@1 track dense within ~1 absolute point on every corpus, and best static α is still beaten on R@1 by large margins. The learned per-query α does not overfit to training-set encoder axes.
